Observation Quantity and Relevance
    1. Observed at least two people
    2. Observed at least three people.
    3. Provides valid explanation about how observed activity is related to brief.
    Observation Breakdowns & Opportunities
    1. Observations clearly demonstrate breakdowns or design opportunities.
    2. Observations were well written, informative, and comprehensive.
    3. Observations were relevant to the brief and activity.
    Photos/Sketches
    1. Submitted photo or sketch #1 and captions demonstrate a breakdown or design opportunity.
    2. Submitted photo or sketch #2 and captions demonstrate a breakdown or design opportunity.
    3. Submitted photo or sketch #3 and captions demonstrate a breakdown or design opportunity.
    Ideation Quantity
    1. Submitted 8+ user needs, excluding redundant or irrelevant needs.
    2. Submitted 15+ user needs, excluding redundant or irrelevant needs.
    Ideation Quality
    1. Needs are human-centered: problem/opportunity should be a deep user need (the underlying issue), rather than a surface need (what people say first).
    2. Needs were articulated as problems/challenges/opportunities, not as solutions or features.
    Needfinding Talk
    1. Student presents findings in studio.
    2. Student is focused and prepared, ending on time.
